# FixMzansi Moat Strategy

## Goal
Build defensibility around trust, supply density, distribution, and data.

## Moat 1: Trust graph
FixMzansi should become the place where verified work history matters.

Compounding signals:
- verified reviews
- job completion history
- response speed
- dispute resolution record
- certificate verification
- repeat customer rate

## Moat 2: Local supply density
Own one geography before expanding.

Strategy:
- start with Cape Town
- focus on solar first
- build deep coverage in the highest-value suburbs
- make the platform feel always available in that zone

## Moat 3: SEO + local landing pages
Each pro, suburb, and service page becomes indexable content.

Examples:
- solar installation Cape Town
- solar installer in Stellenbosch
- battery installation Cape Town
- inverter repair Cape Town
- solar maintenance Cape Town

## Moat 4: WhatsApp-native distribution
Most users already live in WhatsApp.

Use cases:
- booking confirmations
- pro notifications
- review requests
- referral sharing
- rebooking reminders

## Moat 5: Operational excellence
The company should become known for reliability.

This means:
- fast support
- real vetting
- clean communication
- transparent pricing
- dispute handling
- good UX on low-end phones

## Moat 6: Marketplace data
Over time, FixMzansi can learn:
- which jobs happen where
- which services are in highest demand
- price ranges by suburb
- seasonal spikes
- repeat-booking behavior

## Long-term moat ladder
1. verified listings
2. matched bookings
3. repeat customers
4. local SEO dominance
5. payments layer
6. financing layer
7. B2B partnerships
8. service intelligence / pricing data

## Moat statement
FixMzansi should not compete as another directory. It should become the trusted operating layer for solar services in Cape Town.
