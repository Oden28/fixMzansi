# FixMzansi Moat Strategy

## Goal
Build defensibility around trust, supply density, distribution, and data.

## Moat 1: Trust graph
FixMzansi should become the place where verified work history matters.

What compounds:
- verified reviews from completed jobs
- job completion history
- response speed
- dispute resolution record
- certificate verification
- repeat customer rate

Why it matters:
A generic directory can copy listings, but it cannot easily copy accumulated trust.

## Moat 2: Local supply density
Own one geography before expanding.

Strategy:
- start with one metro
- focus on one or two urgent categories
- build deep coverage in suburbs where demand is highest
- make the platform feel "always available" in that zone

Why it matters:
Marketplaces win when users open the app and immediately see relevant options.

## Moat 3: SEO + local landing pages
Every pro, suburb, and service page becomes indexable content.

Examples:
- plumber in Sandton
- electrician in Pretoria East
- geyser repair Johannesburg
- emergency plumbing Randburg

Why it matters:
Search traffic becomes cheaper over time and harder to replicate at scale.

## Moat 4: WhatsApp-native distribution
Most users already live in WhatsApp.

Use cases:
- booking confirmations
- pro notifications
- review requests
- referral sharing
- rebooking reminders

Why it matters:
The product should spread through the channels people already use.

## Moat 5: Operational excellence
The company should become known for reliability.

This means:
- fast support
- real vetting
- clean communication
- transparent pricing
- dispute handling
- good UX on low-end phones

## Moat 6: Marketplace data
Over time, FixMzansi can learn:
- which jobs happen where
- which trades are in highest demand
- price ranges by suburb
- seasonal spikes
- repeat-booking behavior

Why it matters:
This data becomes a product, an internal advantage, and a sales asset.

## Long-term moat ladder
1. verified listings
2. matched bookings
3. repeat customers
4. local SEO dominance
5. payments layer
6. financing layer
7. B2B partnerships
8. service intelligence / pricing data

## Moat statement
FixMzansi should not compete as "another directory". It should become the trusted operating layer for home services in South Africa.
