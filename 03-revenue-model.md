# FixMzansi Revenue Model

## Goal
Create a revenue system that starts simple and expands into multiple high-margin streams.

## Stage 1 revenue: transaction commission
Main model:
- take a commission on completed jobs
- split the burden between consumer and pro if needed
- keep the structure simple and transparent

Why this works:
- aligned with marketplace value creation
- scales with GMV
- easy to understand

## Stage 2 revenue: featured listings
Pros pay for visibility.

Examples:
- boosted profile placement
- suburb priority listing
- urgent-job placement
- profile enhancement pack

This works best once there is meaningful demand.

## Stage 3 revenue: payment and finance take rate
Later, embed financial products:
- card payments
- instant EFT
- deposits / escrow
- pay-in-3 or BNPL
- protection plans

Why this matters:
Financial services increase monetization per transaction.

## Stage 4 revenue: B2B partnerships
Sell the platform as infrastructure to:
- hardware stores
- estate agents
- property managers
- building contractors
- facilities teams

Examples:
- installation referrals
- maintenance bookings
- white-label booking flow
- lead routing

## Stage 5 revenue: subscription products
Once pros rely on the platform, offer subscriptions:
- pro dashboard
- analytics
- lead priority
- rebooking tools
- CRM features
- branded profile pages

## Revenue priorities by phase
### Pre-launch
- no revenue optimization
- focus on trust and liquidity

### Early launch
- small commission
- manual payments if needed
- avoid overcomplication

### Growth stage
- featured listings
- subscriptions
- payment layer

### Scale stage
- B2B deals
- financial products
- data products

## Pricing principle
Never charge in a way that slows liquidity before the marketplace is working.

## Revenue goal
The best version of FixMzansi is a marketplace with multiple monetization layers, but the first dollar should come from completed value, not vanity billing.
